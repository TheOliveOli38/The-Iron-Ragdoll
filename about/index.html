About

Prospit's Requiem is a webcomic by Tofutush.

"A girl called Sparky is sent to a town of left-behind children. She solves a murder mystery which leads her to an even bigger conspiracy, but not before drinking a lot of lemonade and eating a bunch of junk food. Also, everyone has cute ears and superpowers, but they are human."

Also available on MSPFA, Webtoons, and Tapas.

Let me tell you about Homestuck.

Prospit's Requiem is heavily inspired by the webcomic Homestuck, by Andrew Hussie, because I love that comic. You should read it too!

Prospit's Requiem was originally the backstory of a character, also called Sparky, in my Homestuck fan adventure, Forty Donuts. When her backstory developed so, I decided to simply make it into my original comic. The Sparky in Forty Donuts now has a boring mundane backstory now, but I don't regret it.

Though Prospit's Requiem is not a fanwork of Homestuck, it still has a lot of Homestuck elements, such as abundant six-letter names, the aliased, circle-faces art style, and a lot of people wearing gray or black clothes with symbols on them. The bunny ears (and jerboa ears, fox ears...) are a substitute for troll horns, because a lot of character designs look weird without them. (Looking cute is still a big reason for the ears though! And unlike horns, they can flop around to express emotion! DISCLAIMER: I am not a furry. I love drawing cute animals but would freak out if one actually appears in real life.)

What does "Prospit" mean?

Prosperity + respite.

"Prospit" is a made-up word in Homestuck, the name of a kingdom of white chess people on a golden moon that some people wake up on when their actual bodies go to sleep.

Prospit is the complementary of Derse (dearth + terse), the name of a kingdom of black chess people on a purple moon.

I sure hope these names aren't copyrighted. Just one little word is fine, I think? I really couldn't come up with a better idea for this comic.
